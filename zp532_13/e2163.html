Nuri Mono (Lacquerware) Guide

Lacquerware has been cherished as a traditional Japanese art form for centuries. Its elegant and refined luster has seamlessly integrated into Japanese daily life. Historically, lacquer coatings have been found on bowls and containers unearthed from the Jomon period, showing its use for a long time.

Lacquerware, known as "Japan," blends the classical art of Japanese tea culture with modern sensibilities. It continues to be cherished by many as daily necessities and commemorative items.
